Screen 01

Map home — trust at a glance

The daily-use surface. Even users with no complaint open the app to pick where to fill up, which is what keeps the platform alive between incidents.

1

Blend filters are the retention hook

"Premium (non-E20)" and "E10 available" answer the question drivers actually ask every week. Filters read from crowd-updated pumps.blends.

2

Pins carry the score, color carries the verdict

Green ≥ 80, amber 50–79, red < 50. Grey dot = fewer than 5 weighted reports — shown as "Not enough data", never a bad score. No pump is tarred by one review.

3

Every pin is a real dealer

Pumps are seeded from OMC locator lists with dealer codes, so the peek card shows the code that a formal complaint will need later.

Screen 02

Pump page — the public record

Also rendered server-side on the web app, so "<pump name> reviews" searches land here. This page is the acquisition channel.

1

Signals, not stars

The breakdown shows counts of observable signals from a fixed vocabulary. Aggregates of "14 riders reported mileage drop" are defensible; "this pump adulterates" is not. Positive signals are counted with equal weight.

2

Verification is visible

The geo-verified badge shows the capture distance from the pump. Unverified reports still publish, marked and down-weighted — honesty about confidence builds trust.

3

Dealer right-of-reply

A claimed pump gets one response per report, visually distinct. This is the main defamation mitigant and makes the platform read as fair, not adversarial.

Screen 03

Report flow — evidence by construction

Three steps: capture, classify, confirm. The camera is the flow — there is no gallery picker, so every photo is born verified or not at all.

1

Verification happens silently at capture

GPS fix, distance to pump, mock-location flag, and timestamp are recorded when the shutter fires. Within 150 m and uploaded inside 10 minutes → geo_verified. Fails? The report still files, just unverified — never silently discarded.

2

Chips replace the rant box

One tap per observable fact. Free text comes later, optional, capped and moderated — and never feeds the score. "Good experience" sits in the same row: this is a trust index, not a complaint wall.

3

Fill details seed the mileage tracker

Litres, amount, odometer — optional now, but they make phase 2 (km/l trends per pump) work from day one of data collection.

Screen 04

Complaint assistant — prepare, don't submit

CPGRAMS has no public third-party API, and automating a citizen's grievance is legally murky anyway. So the app does everything except press the button.

1

The draft does the hard part

Correct dealer code, OMC, timestamps, and signals — the details that get real grievances actioned instead of closed as "insufficient information". Evidence photos ship as a ZIP from the private bucket.

2

Loop closure is the differentiator

Users paste their registration number back; the app tracks it and nudges at the 30-day CPGRAMS target with the escalation path. Government portals leave this loop open — closing it is why people will use us instead.

3

Rights education rides along

The measure-check and density-test rights are the single highest-leverage fact most drivers don't know. Surfacing it here turns a complaint into on-the-spot verification next time.
